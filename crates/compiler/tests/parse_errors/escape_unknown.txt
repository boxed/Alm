Backslashes always start escaped characters, but I do not recognize this one:

3| x = "\q"
        ^^
Valid escape characters include:

    \n
    \r
    \t
    \"
    \'
    \\
    \u{003D}

Do you want one of those instead? Maybe you need \\ to escape a backslash?

Note: The last style lets encode ANY character by its Unicode code point. That
means \u{0009} and \t are the same. You can use that style for anything not
covered by the other six escapes!
